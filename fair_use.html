Fair Use

Fair use is the copying of copyrighted works legally. You are able to copy copyrighted material for commentary, criticism, and parody. For example if you are writing a review on a book fair use principles allow you to reproduce some of the work to achieve your purposes. Things such as copying a few paragraphs would be acceptable. Parodies are works that mock another persons work. Other fair uses include news reporting, teaching, scholarship, and research. Section 107 of the Copyright Act provides the framework for determining what is fair use. These factors include: purpose and character of the use, nature of the copyrighted work, amount and substantiality of the portion used in relation to the copyrighted work as a whole, and the effect of the use upon the potential market for or value of the copyrighted work.
